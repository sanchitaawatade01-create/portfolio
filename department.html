Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical engineering is the foundational branch of engineering that applies physics, mathematics, and materials science to design, analyze, and manufacture physical machines, mechanisms, and thermal systems. It is the backbone of industries driving modern movement.

Electrical Engineering

Electrical engineering is the branch of engineering that designs and studies systems involving electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It powers modern technology, driving advancements in renewable energy, telecommunications, automation, and electrical grids

Civil Engineering

Civil engineering is a professional discipline focused on the design, construction, and maintenance of the physical and naturally built environment. It shapes modern society by creating the critical infrastructure we rely on daily, including roads, bridges.

Computer Engineering

Computer engineering is a dynamic field that bridges electrical engineering and computer science. It focuses on the design, development, and integration of both computer hardware (e.g., processors, circuits) and software (e.g., operating systems, embedded systems)

Electronic Engineering

Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering (ETE or EnTC) is a dynamic field combining electrical engineering, computer science, and system design. It drives modern digital infrastructure by developing semiconductor devices, 5G/6G networks.

Environment Engineering

Environmental engineering is a branch of engineering focused on protecting human health and improving the natural environment. It applies scientific and mathematical principles to design solutions for air and water pollution control, recycling, waste disposal.
